Refraction, not blur

The sticky nav above uses backdrop-filter: url(#liquid-glass) — an SVG displacement-map lens that bends the backdrop like curved glass, with a subtle rainbow fringe (chromatic dispersion) along its edges. On Safari/Firefox it degrades to a regular frosted blur.

How to spot it

Look at how the diagonal stripes warp and magnify as they pass under the nav while scrolling. The bending is strongest within ~16px of the nav's edge — that is the displacement map's steep edge climb simulating a curved rim.

Chromatic dispersion

The same displacement map is applied three times with slightly different scales (-140 / -124 / -108), once per RGB channel, then screen-blended. The tiny offset between channels produces the rainbow fringe, like light through a prism.

Fallback

Browsers without backdrop-filter: url() support get blur(12px) saturate(1.5) via an @supports query in liquid-glass.css — same element, no JS feature detection needed.
